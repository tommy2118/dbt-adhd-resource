Expectation Thinking Traps

The expectations we place on ourselves and others can lead to unhelpful thinking patterns.

About Expectation Traps

Expectation traps are thinking patterns related to the unrealistic or rigid expectations we place on ourselves, others, and the world. These patterns often lead to disappointment, frustration, and relationship conflicts.

Key Takeaway

By identifying and challenging your expectations, you can develop more flexible thinking that leads to greater acceptance and reduced emotional suffering.

Common Expectation Traps

Should Statements

Using rigid rules about how you, others, or the world "should," "must," or "ought to" be.

Mind Reading

Assuming you know what others are thinking without sufficient evidence.

Learn More →

Fortune Telling

Predicting negative outcomes without considering other possibilities or evidence.

Learn More →

Just World Fallacy

Believing that the world is fair and people get what they deserve.

Fallacy of Change

Expecting that others will change to suit our needs if we pressure or encourage them enough.

Heaven's Reward Fallacy

Expecting that self-sacrifice and self-denial will eventually pay off, as if someone is keeping score.

DBT Skills for Expectation Traps

These DBT skills can help you challenge and overcome expectation-related thinking traps:

Radical Acceptance

Accept reality as it is, rather than fighting against what "should" be. This reduces suffering caused by unrealistic expectations.

Effective Interpersonal Skills

Learn to express needs directly and validate others, rather than expecting them to read your mind or meet unstated expectations.
